Visitors to Dominica normally do not require a visa, unless they are nationals of the Dominican Republic or Haiti.[1]

Visa policy map

[edit]

Visa exemption

[edit]

Freedom of movement

[edit]

As of 1 October, 2025 the four nations of: The Commonwealth of Dominica, Barbados, Belize, and Saint Vincent and the Grenadines offer the right of indefinite stay for their aforementioned citizens.

Nationals of any other country except nationals of the Dominican Republic and Haiti can visit visa free for 21 days.

Date of visa abolition
  • 21 December 1987: Israel[6]
  • 11 March 1993: for 21 days: citizens of any countries.[7]
  • 11 March 1993: for 6 months: Argentina, Belgium, Costa Rica, Denmark, France, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Israel, Italy, Japan, Luxembourg, Malta, Mexico, Netherlands (including Netherlands Antilles), Norway, Portugal, South Korea, Spain, Suriname, Sweden, Taiwan, United Kingdom, United States and Venezuela.[8]
  • 25 July 2013: for 6 months: Austria, Bulgaria, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Estonia, Finland, Hungary, Latvia, Lithuania, Poland, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia.[9]
  • 20 November 2014: for 3 months: Brazil[10]
  • 19 March 2015: for 28 days: Cuba[11]
  • 21 May 2015: for 90 days: Uruguay[12]
  • 13 August 2015: for 6 months: Croatia and Switzerland[13]
  • 18 February 2016: for 6 months: Iceland and Liechtenstein[14]
  • 14 January 2019: for 90 days: Russia[15]
  • 13 November 2019: for 90 days: Ukraine[16]
  • 17 July 2025: for 90 days: Tajikistan[17]

Cancelled:

  • 1 December 2005: Dominican Republic[18]
  • 7 October 2019: Haiti[19]

Nationals of France with ID card can visit visa free for a maximum stay of 2 weeks.

Nationals of Germany and United Kingdom with ID Card can visit visa-free for a maximum stay of 1 week.
